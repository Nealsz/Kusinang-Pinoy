About Kusinang Pinoy

Welcome to Kusinang Pinoy! Discover the vibrant world of Filipino cuisine with Kusinang Pinoy, your ultimate destination for authentic Filipino recipes. Whether you're a seasoned cook or just starting out, our collection of traditional dishes will guide you through the unique flavors and culinary traditions of the Philippines. We believe that food is more than just sustenance—it's a celebration of culture, community, and family. Our recipes are easy to follow and prepare in your own kitchen, with step-by-step instructions, tips, and insights into the ingredients and techniques. From the comforting warmth of Adobo to the legendary taste of Sinigang, each recipe will delight your senses and enrich your culinary repertoire. Tumuklas, Magluto at Magalak — Maligayang Pagdating sa Kusinang Pinoy!

Featured Recipes

Pork Adobo

A savory and tangy dish made with pork marinated in vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, bay leaves, and black peppercorns, then slow-cooked until tender. It is often served with rice.

Sinigang na Baboy

A sour soup typically made with pork with a tangy flavor derived from tamarind. It includes vegetables such as kangkong (water spinach), radish, tomatoes, and eggplant, making for a refreshing and hearty dish.

View Recipe

Tinola

comforting chicken soup made with chicken pieces simmered in a ginger-based broth, green papaya (also known as sayote), and leafy greens like chili pepper leaves or spinach. It is flavored with fish sauce and known for its simple yet aromatic and flavorful broth.

View Recipe

Kare-Kare

A rich and creamy stew made with oxtail, tripe, and vegetables such as eggplant, banana blossom, and string beans. The sauce is made from ground peanuts, rice, and annatto seeds. It's traditionally served with bagoong (fermented shrimp paste).

View Recipe

Pancit Palabok

Noodle dish topped with a rich and flavorful orange sauce made from shrimp broth, annatto, and flour. It is garnished with shrimp, crushed chicharrón, hard-boiled eggs, green onions, and fried garlic. A squeeze of calamansi adds a tangy touch before serving.

View Recipe

Bicol Express

A spicy dish made from pork stewed in coconut milk with shrimp paste and an abundance of chili peppers. It's rich, creamy, and known for its fiery kick, typical of Bicolano cuisine.

View Recipe

Lumpiang Shanghai

A type of Filipino spring roll filled with a mixture of ground pork, minced onions, carrots, and seasonings. The rolls are wrapped in a thin lumpia wrapper and deep-fried until golden and crispy. They are typically served with a sweet and sour dipping sauce.

View Recipe

Chicken Inasal

A type of grilled chicken marinated in a mixture of calamansi, vinegar, pepper, and annatto for a distinctive orange hue. It’s typically grilled over hot coals and served with rice, often flavored with chicken oil. It’s known for its savory and tangy flavor.
